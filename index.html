I created my 3D chair modeling utilizing four new modeling methods we haven't learned yet: a background image, beveling, the mirror modifier, and bezier curves. I first started with utilizing a background image for inspiration. Then after I found one I liked I opened up the right side bar menu in the viewport and added it to the right view for perspective mode.

After I added that image I began modeling the center of the chair and the legs. For the legs I created a rectangle (through scaling a cube), and then I added a mirror modifier to reflect the leg evenly around the orign in the x and y directions. A problem I ran into doing this was that the legs were not reflecting properly at first. To fix this I moved the object's origin point (the orange dot) to the center of the world so that the legs had a correct reference point to reflect from.


Next I utilized beveling (control b) to make the ends of the legs look smoother. After that I saw that the chair in the picture had curved rims, and I wanted to duplicate that effect. To do that I created a bezier curve object and I moved the two arrows around through translation and scaling until I got the picture below. I also utilized the mirror modifier with clipping to make the curve reflect to a second half without it overlaping itself.
